Cities and local policies key to overcome hunger, stresses head of UN agency

José Graziano da Silva, the Director General of the UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), highlights the critical role of cities in addressing malnutrition through innovative partnerships and actions. He emphasizes the need for collaboration between urban centers and diverse stakeholders to combat food waste and ensure access to nutritious diets for all individuals. Graziano da Silva cites examples of successful initiatives, such as Brazil's Zero Hunger Program, where cities played a pivotal role in providing affordable, nutritious meals and supporting local food production. Additionally, he discusses FAO's support for implementing the Milan Urban Food Policy Pact and emphasizes aligning efforts with the New Urban Agenda for sustainable urban development. Overall, the main idea is the importance of urban involvement and collaboration in addressing malnutrition and promoting food security.

Visiting UN food relief agency, Pope Francis shines spotlight on urgent need to end hunger

Pope Francis visited the United Nations World Food Programme (WFP) headquarters in Rome, where he expressed his full support for the organization's goal of eradicating hunger. During his visit, he praised the dedication of WFP staff, honored those who lost their lives in service, and emphasized the importance of continuing the fight against hunger. Pope Francis also addressed issues such as food waste, the impact of conflict on food distribution, and the need for increased global cooperation to address hunger effectively. Additionally, the text highlights the significance of the Pope's visit in bringing attention to the urgent need to end hunger and aligning with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly Goal 2: Zero Hunger, by the year 2030.

WFP boosts efforts to end hunger by 2030 with Indian partnerships

United Nations World Food Programme (WFP) is actively collaborating with the Indian Government and the business sector to enhance nutrition and food security in India, the world's second most populous country. This collaboration is seen as a crucial step towards achieving the goal of eradicating global hunger by 2030, as outlined in the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. WFP Executive Director Ertharin Cousin's visit to India involved signing agreements and engaging with government officials and business leaders to establish initiatives such as a Centre of Excellence on Food and Nutrition Security and partnerships to address malnutrition and food insecurity. Additionally, WFP provides support to the Indian government's food-based social safety nets and implements initiatives such as rice fortification to combat anaemia among school children. Overall, the main idea is the concerted effort between WFP, the Indian Government, and the private sector to improve food and nutrition security in India, contributing to the global goal of Zero Hunger by 2030.

Our Ocean Conference

European Union organized the 4th edition of the "Our Ocean Conference" in Malta, focusing on various interconnected areas related to ocean sustainability. These areas include climate change, marine protected areas, sustainable fisheries, marine pollution, sustainable blue economy, and maritime security. During the conference, numerous commitments were announced by governments, NGOs, foundations, research institutes, international organizations, and the business community to address these issues. The commitments include financial pledges, the establishment of Marine Protected Areas, and various initiatives aimed at reducing plastic pollution and promoting sustainable practices in the ocean economy. The conference served as a platform for stakeholders to come together, discuss, and make tangible commitments towards preserving the health and vitality of the world's oceans.
