What are SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global objectives established by the United Nations in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. These goals serve as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all people by the year 2030.

Origins of the SDGs

OThe Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) emerged from a collective recognition of the urgent need to address pressing global challenges and create a more sustainable and equitable world. Building upon the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs), which were in place from 2000 to 2015, the SDGs were developed through an extensive consultation process involving governments, civil society organizations, businesses, and citizens from around the world.

Impact of the SDGs

TSince their adoption in 2015, the SDGs have catalyzed action and sparked progress towards a more sustainable future. They have inspired governments to integrate sustainable development into their policies and strategies, mobilized resources for sustainable development initiatives, and empowered communities to take ownership of their own development.
