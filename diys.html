Plastic Bottle Planters

1. Cut the top off the plastic bottle.

2. Poke holes in the bottom for drainage.

3. Fill with soil and plant your desired greenery.

4. Hang or place in a sunny spot.

Plastic Bag Jump Rope

1. Cut plastic bags into long strips.

2. Tie them together to make a jump rope.

3. Braid the strips for added durability.

Plastic Bottle Bird Feeder

1. Cut windows or openings in a plastic bottle large enough for birds to access.

2. Fill the bottle with birdseed and hang it outside.

3. This DIY provides food for birds while repurposing plastic bottles.

Plastic Lid Coasters

1. Collect plastic lids from containers like yogurt or milk jugs.

2. Arrange them in a pattern and glue them together to create coasters.

3. Seal the surface with clear varnish or Mod Podge for durability.

Plastic Spoon Mirror Frame

1. Gather plastic spoons and cut off the handles.

2. Glue the spoon heads in overlapping layers around the edge of a mirror or picture frame.

3. Paint the spoons in a uniform color for a stylish and unique frame.

Plastic Bottle Sprinkler

1. Poke holes in a plastic bottle using a hot nail or drill.

2. Attach a hose connector to the bottle opening.

3. Connect it to a garden hose and use it as a DIY sprinkler for watering plants.

Plastic Straw Bracelets

1. Cut plastic straws into small pieces.

2. String them onto elastic cord or thread to make colorful bracelets or necklaces.

3. It's a fun craft project for kids and a creative way to reuse straws.

Plastic Bag Crochet or Knitting

1. Cut plastic bags into strips and tie them together to make "plarn" (plastic yarn).

2. Use plarn for crocheting or knitting projects like bags, mats, or even hats.

3. It's a sustainable way to repurpose plastic bags into functional items.
